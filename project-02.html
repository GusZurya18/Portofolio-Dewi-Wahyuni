Project Overview

Real skin, real truth

An authentic digital series providing transparent insights into trending Indonesian beauty products. This project focuses on showing the real performance of a product — helping followers understand exactly how it looks on real skin before they spend their money.

In a market flooded with filtered swatches and brand-sponsored content, this series fills a critical gap: unedited, close-up documentation of how products actually perform on real skin, real lighting, and real texture — not the idealized version you see in ads.

Products reviewed span makeup, skincare, and haircare from local brands like Miranda, Emina, Wardah, Implora, Hanasui, Glad2Glow, and more — all tested personally over 1–2 weeks before any verdict is given.

Problem & Solution

The Challenge & The Fix

📵 The Challenge

Camera lies — and followers can't feel the texture

Mobile phone cameras often "wash out" colors, making a vibrant blush or lipstick look pale and dull on screen. Worse, no video can communicate whether a product feels sticky, powdery, oily, or weightless — yet that sensory detail is exactly what determines whether someone will love or regret a purchase. Followers were left guessing, and trust in online beauty reviews was breaking down.

🎠 The Solution

The "Comparison & Texture" Carousel — a sensory system in slides

I created a two-part carousel system: Slide A shows a heavy, exaggerated swatch to capture the product's true pigment that cameras normally wash out. Slide B shows the same product blended realistically on skin. Text overlays throughout describe the skin-feel — words like "weightless," "non-sticky," "velvet-dry" — giving followers a complete sensory picture without ever touching the product themselves.

Review Methodology

How I Review

01
Product Testing — Minimum 1 Week
Every product is used daily for at least 1–2 weeks before filming. No first-impression reviews — only real long-term performance counts. Worn in different conditions: humid weather, full-day work, outdoor lighting.
02
Pigment & Texture Mapping
Before filming, I document the product's true pigment with a heavy exaggerated swatch against various skin tones, and write a sensory vocabulary list — specific words that describe how it feels (powdery, tacky, melts in, sits on top). This becomes the overlay copy.
03
Filming the Comparison Carousel
Shot in consistent natural window light with no beauty filters. Slide A: exaggerated swatch. Slide B: realistic blended look. Slide C+: texture description overlays, longevity shots, packaging close-ups, and the final verdict frame.
04
Verdict: Holy Grail · Worth It · Skip It
Every review ends with a clear 3-tier verdict system. "Holy Grail" = repurchase. "Worth It" = solid buy. "Skip It" = not worth your money. Plus a "Best Suited For" note so followers know if the product matches their skin type.

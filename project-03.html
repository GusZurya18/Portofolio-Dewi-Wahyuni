Project Overview

Clear skin, real confidence

A dedicated educational campaign focused on helping teenagers navigate the challenges of puberty-related skin concerns — specifically oily skin and acne. This project simplifies complex skincare concepts into relatable, "safe-for-teens" routines that prioritize long-term skin health over instant but potentially harmful results.

The core mission: stop teens from reaching for harsh viral products that damage their skin barrier and make breakouts worse. Replace fear, confusion, and misinformation with a simple, empowering 3-step system they can actually follow every day — affordably.

Every piece of content is designed to feel like advice from a trusted older friend, not a lecture from a dermatologist — warm, relatable, and always encouraging.

Problem & Solution

The Challenge & The Fix

⚠️ The Challenge

Viral harsh products destroying teen skin barriers — and confidence

High school students with oily skin often fall for "viral" products with harsh ingredients — high-percentage acids, alcohol-heavy toners, and scrubs — that strip and damage the skin barrier. The result: even more breakouts, redness, and irritation than before. On top of the physical damage, acne takes a serious toll on self-confidence in a school environment where appearance feels magnified. Teens needed simple, safe guidance — not trend-chasing skincare.

🛡️ The Solution

The "Acne-Safe Survival" Carousel — simple, affordable, empowering

I developed the Acne-Safe Survival Carousel — a step-by-step visual system built around the "Basic Skincare" method: cleanse, moisturize, protect. Using Step-by-Step Visual Hacks, I teach teens to identify acne-safe (non-comedogenic) ingredients on product labels, so they can shop confidently without needing a dermatologist. The message is always: managing oily skin can be simple, affordable, and deeply empowering.

The Acne-Safe Method

Basic Skincare 3-Step System

01
🧼
Cleanse
The Foundation
Use a gentle, low-pH face wash twice daily. No foaming scrubs — they strip oil and trigger rebound sebum production. Look for: Salicylic Acid 0.5–1% or Centella Asiatica for acne-prone skin.
✓ Non-comedogenic ✓ Low-pH ✗ No alcohol
→
02
💧
Moisturize
The Myth-Breaker
Oily skin still needs moisture — skipping this step triggers more oil. Use a lightweight, water-based gel moisturizer. Key ingredients: Niacinamide (controls sebum), Hyaluronic Acid (hydrates without weight).
✓ Oil-free ✓ Gel formula ✗ No heavy cream
→
03
☀️
Protect
The Non-Negotiable
SPF every morning — even on cloudy days or indoors. UV exposure worsens post-acne dark spots. Choose SPF 30+ sunscreen with matte finish. Many teens skip this step: it's the one that matters most for long-term skin health.
✓ SPF 30+ ✓ Matte finish ✗ No skipping

Content Approach

How I Teach

01
Identify the Viral Misinformation
Monitor trending skincare content on TikTok and Instagram to find which viral products and routines are actively harming teen skin. Real comment pain points become the hook for each carousel.
02
Build the Visual Hack Carousel
Design each carousel slide as a standalone visual hack — clear, screenshot-able, and actionable without needing to watch a video. The Acne-Safe Survival carousel maps ingredients, steps, and product recommendations in a single swipeable guide.
03
Film: Relatable, Not Clinical
Every video uses conversational language, no lab coat, no complicated terminology. Show real skin in real lighting — including breakouts — to normalize the experience and break down the shame that stops teens from seeking real help.
04
Build Community in Comments
Actively reply to every question in comments. Skin concerns feel personal and isolating — responding by name makes teens feel seen and builds a safe learning space where they return for more education.
